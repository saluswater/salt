1. Introduction

Keeping your water treatment system running properly is essential to ensure quality water in your home. This guide will help you understand how to replenish salt efficiently and effectively.

2. When to Replenish Salt

When to replenish salt?

  • Check the level at least once a month.
  • Replenishment should be done whenever salt is below half of the compartment.
  • Never let the reservoir run completely out of salt.
  • If you can see the bottom of the salt tank, it's past time to refill.

3. Choosing the Right Type of Salt

What salt should I use?

There are different salt options for your water treatment system. The ideal choice depends on availability in your region and your specific preferences.

Preferred Option:

Morton Professional Water Softener Crystals

  • 100% natural salt with high purity
  • Available in 50 lbs (22.6 kg) bags

  • Ideal for most water treatment systems

Important:

Regardless of the option chosen, make sure the salt is specific for water treatment systems and preferably in pellet or crystal format to ensure proper dissolution.

4. Why Salt is Important

Why is salt important?

  • Salt doesn't filter water. It's used to backwash the minerals that do the purification.
  • These minerals remove chlorine, heavy metals, and impurities from water.
  • Without salt, the system loses efficiency and water returns to having hardness and contamination.

5. How to Replenish

How to replenish?

  • Open the salt reservoir lid (located next to the external system).
  • Check if the level is below half.
  • Pour 1 bag at a time directly into the reservoir.
  • No need to wash the compartment beforehand.
  • Fill until reaching about ¾ of the tank capacity.
  • Close the lid tightly after refilling.
  • It's normal to have water in the reservoir. Salt needs to be immersed to work properly.

6. Recommended Frequency

Recommended Frequency

  • 1 bag per month.
  • Or 2 bags every 2 months.

7. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use any type of salt in my system?
No. It's important to use only salt specific for water treatment systems, like the ones recommended in this guide. Table salt or other types can damage your system.
Is it normal to have water in the salt reservoir?
Yes, it's completely normal. Salt needs to be partially immersed in water to create the brine necessary for the mineral regeneration process.
What happens if I forget to replenish the salt?
If the salt runs out completely, the system won't be able to regenerate the filtration minerals. This will result in hard water and possible contaminants returning to circulate in your home.
What's the difference between salt types?
The main difference is in additives and benefits. Green salt (5-in-1) offers an additional benefit of improving water taste, while yellow salt (4-in-1) and blue salt (Professional) have different levels of purity and dissolution.
